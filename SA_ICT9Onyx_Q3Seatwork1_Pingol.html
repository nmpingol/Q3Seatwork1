Gilmore Girls

Analysis

Sypnosis

Gilmore Girls is a TV show that aired during 2000-2007. It follows a mother and daughter duo as we watch them grow and learn. This show has dynamic characters who go through their own unique challenges. The creators aren't afraid to tackle controversial issues while at the same time having a lot of comedy. The story takes place in Stars Hollow, a fictional small town where everyone knows each other. The setting of the show, especially in the first season, make the show a fall classic.

Characters

Lorelai Gilmore

Protrayed by Lauren Graham, Lorelai is the mother of Rory and she runs the Independence Inn. Since childhood, she didn't have a very good relationship with her parents at home. Her mother was very controlling and judgemental while her father had all of his focus on work. She had her daughter when she was 16, and ran away from her parents house in Hartford to a humble house in Stars Hollow. Even though she has a privelleged background, she worked her way up to where she is now. From being a maid at the Independence Inn to owning it. She raised her daughter as a single mother. Often praising her, making her daughter believe she is the best. The duo are known to have electric personalites, be fast talkers, and food lovers.

Rory Gilmore

Played by Alexis Bledel, she is the loved daughter of Lorelai Gilmore. She is constantly being praised by her mother, her neighbours, and her grandparents. This unfortunately gives her a golden child or superiority complex mindset. She studies at Chilton High School. She is known to read a lot of books. It was also mentioned that ever since she was very young, she wanted to go to Harvard in college. In the first season, we can see that she is kind of a perfectionist. She often has lists, is very organized, and always studies. She cannot handle making mistakes or falling behind even just a bit.

Emily Gilmore

Played by Kelly Bishop, she is Lorelai's mother and Rory's grandmother. She is a wealthy socialite who lives in Hartfort with her husband. A funny trait about her is that she fires her maids all the time, so she has a different maid every week. She finds showing emotions as a weakness, in turn she often avoids being vulnerable or compassionate to her family. Throwing money at people seems to be the way she shows love. Throughout the series, we can see the dynamic of her relationship with her daughter and grandaughter.

Richard Gilmore

Played by Edward Herrmann, he is a typical father/grandfather character. He is often seen in a suit, reading the newspaper, or smoking a cigar. He is a business man who has his focus on work most of the time, sadly neglecting his loved ones. Though he is strict and formal, he has a softer character than Emily. He can show affection towards his grandaughter and he defends his daughter when she is being targetted.

Dean Forester

Played by Jared Padalecki, he was a boy who recently moved to Stars Hollow then became Rory's first boyfriend. At first he was a sweet boyfriend who would give handmade gifts and sweet gestures. Later on, he starts being more controlling or needy. His relationship with Rory is a typical high school romance, realistically protrayed in the show.

Jess Mariano

Played by Milo Ventimiglia, he is Luke's nephew. Though he is not intentionally trying to be a bad infulence, he is villanized by the townspeople and always viewed in a bad light. He is said to mirror Rory since he is also smart and a book lover.

Luke Danes

Played by Scott Patterson, he is the owner of Luke's Diner. This diner is loved in Stars Hollow, especially by Lorelai and Rory who visit it everyday to drink coffee or eat a meal. Luke is a lovable character because he seems though or strict on the outside, but he is secretly soft and affectionate. Though they are "just friends", he finds himself comforting and being there for Lorelai when she needs someone.
